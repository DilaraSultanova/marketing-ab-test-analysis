# Marketing A/B Test Analysis

## Overview

This project analyzes a marketing A/B testing dataset containing over 588,000 users. The objective was to compare conversion performance between an advertisement campaign and a Public Service Announcement (PSA) control group. Using Microsoft Excel, Pivot Tables, and dashboard visualization techniques, the analysis identifies which campaign generated a higher conversion rate and provides actionable business insights.

---

## Dataset

- Source: Kaggle Marketing A/B Testing Dataset
- Records Analyzed: 588,101 users
- Groups:
  - Advertisement Group (Ad)
  - PSA Control Group

## Business Question

Does the advertisement campaign produce a higher conversion rate than the PSA control group?

---

## Results

| Group | Users | Conversions | Conversion Rate |
|---------|---------:|------------:|---------------:|
| Ad | 564,577 | 14,423 | 2.55% |
| PSA | 23,524 | 420 | 1.79% |

### Key Findings

- The advertisement group achieved a conversion rate of **2.55%**.
- The PSA group achieved a conversion rate of **1.79%**.
- The advertisement campaign generated approximately **43% higher conversion performance** than the PSA group.
- Users exposed to advertisements were more likely to convert than users exposed to the PSA campaign.

## Conclusion

The advertisement campaign significantly outperformed the PSA control group, achieving a higher conversion rate and generating stronger marketing performance. The results suggest that the advertising strategy was effective in driving user conversions and should be considered for future campaigns.
